# Disputes and Chargebacks

## Dispute vs Chargeback
A dispute is a customer questioning a charge through ESTC support, resolved
internally by verifying usage and applying credits. A chargeback is initiated by
the customer's bank, which forcibly reverses the charge and notifies the payment
processor. Chargebacks carry processor fees and are handled with higher urgency.

## Handling a Chargeback
When a chargeback is received, the account is flagged and automatic billing is
paused to prevent compounding disputes. The support specialist gathers evidence:
the signed contract, usage logs, and prior correspondence, and submits it to the
processor within the response deadline, which is typically 7 to 10 calendar days.

## Outcome
If the chargeback is won, the original charge stands and the account flag is
cleared. If lost, the amount is written off and the account may be required to
switch to prepaid billing. Repeated chargebacks can result in account suspension.

## Preventing Disputes
Clear invoice line items, advance dunning notices, and proactive outage credits
reduce dispute volume. Support specialists should always confirm a customer has
seen the relevant invoice before escalating to a formal dispute.

## Refund Interaction
A charge that is already under chargeback cannot also be refunded through the normal
flow; the refund must wait for the chargeback to resolve to avoid a double reversal.
